Check your inbox for the 10 hacks and The Synchrony Advantage study. While you're here — here's a quick preview:

  1. 1
    Start every meeting with a 60-second check-in
    "How am I showing up today?" Shared state = faster sync.
  2. 2
    Name the decision before you discuss it
    Write at the top of every agenda: "The decision we need to make is ___." Eliminates 40% of meeting drift.
  3. 3
    Close every meeting with one action per person
    One specific commitment, one owner, one deadline. Read it back out loud.
  4. 4
    Rotate who speaks first
    Whoever goes first anchors the group. Changing the order changes the outcome.
  5. 5
    Use "plus one" acknowledgment
    "I heard you say X — plus one thing I'd add..." Reduces misalignment instantly.
  6. 6
    Create a shared friction log
    A running doc: "This slowed me down today." Review weekly. Fix the top item.
  7. 7
    Set a weekly 15-minute blockers-only standup
    No status updates — only what's stuck and who can help. Hard cap at 15 min.
  8. 8
    Separate idea generation from evaluation
    Never critique in the same session you generate. One meeting to open, one to close.
  9. 9
    Ask "what do you need from me?" once a week
    Each manager to each direct report. "A decision by Thursday" not "support."
  10. 10
    Celebrate a synchrony win out loud
    "We made that call in 10 minutes because we trusted each other." Culture compounds when you name it.
